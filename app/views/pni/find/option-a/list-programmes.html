Building Choices: high intensity

Building Choices helps people to develop skills for change and future-focused goals, to support them in building a crime-free life. It is a cognitive-behavioural programme, combining group and one-to-one sessions. It develops skills such as emotion management, healthy thinking, healthy relationships, sense of purpose and healthy sex. This is the high intensity pathway, including Getting Ready.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Any
Risk criteria

High or very high in at least one of these areas:

  • OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3)
  • OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • OSP-C or OSP-I (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor – Contact or Indecent Imagery)
  • SNSV (Serious Non-Sexual Violence)

Or at least medium SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment)

Needs criteria
High need. This is based on their programme needs score
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Around 17 to 25 weeks (2 to 3 sessions a week)

Building Choices: moderate intensity

Building Choices helps people to develop skills for change and future-focused goals, to support them in building a crime-free life. It is a cognitive-behavioural programme, combining group and one-to-one sessions. It develops skills such as emotion management, healthy thinking, healthy relationships, sense of purpose and healthy sex. This is the moderate intensity pathway.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Any
Risk criteria

Medium in at least one of these areas:

  • OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3)
  • OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • OSP-C or OSP-I (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor – Contact or Indecent Imagery)
  • SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment)
  • SNSV (Serious Non-Sexual Violence)

If the person meets any of these risk criteria, next check the needs criteria.

Needs criteria
Medium need for OGRS, OVP, OSP and SNSV. At least low need and medium risk for SARA. This is based on their programme needs score
Time to complete
Around 10 to 13 weeks (2 to 3 sessions a week)
