Recommended Accredited Programmes

These programmes are recommended for people with high or very high risks and needs.

Becoming New Me Plus: general violence offence

General violence offence

Becoming New Me Plus (BNM+) uses cognitive behavioural therapy. It is for men who are at high or very high risk of reoffending. This strand is for men convicted of a general violence offence.

Setting
Custody
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
  • High OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • High OGRS (Offender Group Reconviction Scale)
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Equivalent non-LDC programme
Kaizen
Time to complete
At least 12 months

Building Choices: high intensity

Building Choices helps people to develop skills for change and future-focused goals. It is a cognitive-behavioural programme, combining group and one-to-one sessions. It develops skills such as emotion management, healthy thinking, healthy relationships, sense of purpose and healthy sex. This is the high intensity pathway, including Getting Ready.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Any, depending on prison requirements
Risk criteria

High or very high in at least one of these areas:

  • OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3)
  • OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • OSP-DC or OSP-IIC (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor – Contact or Indecent Imagery)
  • RSR (Risk of Serious Recidivism)
  • SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment)
Needs criteria
High need, based on their programme needs score
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Around 17 to 25 weeks (2 to 3 sessions a week)

Kaizen: intimate partner violence offence

Intimate partner violence offence

Kaizen uses a unified approach, focusing on the needs of participants. It is inclusive and effective for a range of responsivity needs, including difficulties with engagement, therapeutic relationships and denial. This strand is for men convicted of an intimate partner violence offence.

Setting
Custody
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
High SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment) against partners
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
No
Equivalent non-LDC programmes
Becoming New Me Plus
Time to complete
At least 6 months
