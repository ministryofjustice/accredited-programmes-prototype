Recommended: moderate intensity Accredited Programmes

These programmes are recommended for people with medium risks and needs.

I need to refer to a different programme

If you think the person needs a programme not listed here, you can see non-recommended programmes and request an override. You will need to give a reason for this before you submit the referral.

See high intensity programmes

Building Better Relationships

Intimate partner violence offence

Building Better Relationships (BBR) uses cognitive behavioural therapy. It is for men convicted of an intimate partner violence-related (IPV) offence against a female partner.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
  • Medium SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment) in custody
  • Medium or high SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment) in the community
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
At least 6 months

Building Choices: moderate intensity

Building Choices helps people to develop skills for change and future-focused goals. It is a cognitive-behavioural programme, combining group and one-to-one sessions. It develops skills such as emotion management, healthy thinking, healthy relationships, sense of purpose and healthy sex. This is the moderate intensity pathway.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Any, depending on prison requirements
Risk criteria

Medium in at least one of these areas:

  • OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3)
  • OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • OSP-DC or OSP-IIC (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor – Contact or Indecent Imagery)
  • RSR (Risk of Serious Recidivism)
  • SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment)

The person should not score high in any risk areas for the moderate intensity pathway.

Needs criteria
Medium need for OGRS, OVP, OSP and SNSV. At least low need for SARA. This is based on their programme needs score.
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Around 10 to 13 weeks (2 to 3 sessions a week)

Horizon

Sexual offence

Horizon is for men convicted of a sexual or sexually-motivated offence who are medium risk or above. It helps address problematic factors and how they contribute to behaviour.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria

Medium, high or very high OSP (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor)

Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
No
Equivalent LDC programme
Becoming New Me Plus
Time to complete
At least 6 months (custody); at least 12 months (community)

Identity Matters

Gang-related offence

Identity Matters (IM) is for men whose offending was motivated or enabled by their affiliation to a particular group. It aims to change the nature of their relationship to the group and helps them develop skills to resist peer influence.

Setting
Custody
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
  • Medium or high ROSH (Risk of Serious Harm)
  • 50+ OGRS (Offender Group Reconviction Scale)
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Assessed case by case
Time to complete
Flexible delivery with up to 23 sessions plus pre-programme session and programme review. Around 25 hours (at least 3 months).

Living as New Me

All offences

Living as New Me (LNM) is a booster or maintenance programme for men who have completed New Me Strengths or Becoming New Me Plus and may need extra support. In a custody setting, it is for men convicted of any offence. In the community, it is for sexual offences only.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria

Medium or above risk

Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Rolling programme with at least 5 sessions, often more

New Me MOT

All offences

New Me MOT (NMMOT) is not an Accredited Programme, but is for men who have already completed Becoming New Me Plus, Kaizen, Healthy Sex Programme, Horizon, iHorizon, New Me Strengths, the Cognitive Self-Change Programme or the Self-Change Programme. It offers support throughout a person’s sentence, set out as a series of short exercises. These are completed with support from the person's Prison Offender Manager in custody or their Probation Practitioner in the community. This gives them the chance to practise skills in different contexts, generalising and maintaining their learning from the Accredited Programme.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria

All risk scores

Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Number of sessions is at the discretion of the practitioner and depends on the risk, need and responsivity profile of the individual. Has a 30-day Rehabilitation Activity Requirement (RAR) in the community.

New Me Strengths: general violence offence

General violence offence

New Me Strengths (NMS) is a cognitive-behavioural programme for men at medium risk of reconviction. In a custody setting, it is for men convicted of any offence. In the community, it is for sexual offences only. It helps people recognise the impact and consequences of offending and avoid activities that encourage offending behaviours.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
  • Medium OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • 50+ OGRS (Offender Group Reconviction Scale)
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
At least 6 months

Thinking Skills Programme

All offences

Thinking Skills Programme (TSP) helps men and women to develop skills in pro-social problem solving, perspective taking, developing and managing relationships, and self-management. Suitable for all offences – for sexual offences, consider Horizon or Kaizen first.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male or female
Risk criteria

50+ OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3) over 2 years

Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
No
Equivalent non-LDC programmes
New Me Strengths
Time to complete
At least 6 months
