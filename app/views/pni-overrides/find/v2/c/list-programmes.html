Accredited Programmes: not recommended

Accredited Programmes are not recommended for people who may not be eligible based on risks and needs, like Del Hatton.

You can make a referral anyway and the treatment manager will decide whether an override is appropriate. You will need to give a reason before you submit the referral.

Building Choices: high intensity

Building Choices helps people to develop skills for change and future-focused goals. It is a cognitive-behavioural programme, combining group and one-to-one sessions. It develops skills such as emotion management, healthy thinking, healthy relationships, sense of purpose and healthy sex. This is the high intensity pathway, including Getting Ready.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Any, depending on prison requirements
Risk criteria

High or very high in at least one of these areas:

  • OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3)
  • OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • OSP-DC or OSP-IIC (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor – Contact or Indecent Imagery)
  • RSR (Risk of Serious Recidivism)
  • SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment)
Needs criteria
High need, based on their programme needs score
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Around 17 to 25 weeks (2 to 3 sessions a week)

Building Choices: moderate intensity

Building Choices helps people to develop skills for change and future-focused goals. It is a cognitive-behavioural programme, combining group and one-to-one sessions. It develops skills such as emotion management, healthy thinking, healthy relationships, sense of purpose and healthy sex. This is the moderate intensity pathway.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Any, depending on prison requirements
Risk criteria

Medium in at least one of these areas:

  • OGRS3 (Offender Group Reconviction Scale 3)
  • OVP (OASys Violence Predictor)
  • OSP-DC or OSP-IIC (OASys Sexual Reconviction Predictor – Contact or Indecent Imagery)
  • RSR (Risk of Serious Recidivism)
  • SARA (Spousal Assault Risk Assessment)

The person should not score high in any risk areas for the moderate intensity pathway.

Needs criteria
Medium need for OGRS, OVP, OSP and SNSV. At least low need for SARA. This is based on their programme needs score.
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Around 10 to 13 weeks (2 to 3 sessions a week)

Control of Violence for Angry Impulsive Drinkers

General violence offence

Control of Violence for Angry Impulsive Drinkers (COVAID) is a cognitive-behavioural programme for people who are violent under the influence of alcohol. It encourages them to understand their behaviour and practise skills for change. It combines 10 group sessions with individual support sessions.

Setting
Custody
Gender
Separate male and female programmes
Risk criteria
  • Score of under 20 on AUDIT questionnaire
  • Medium (35 to 75%) OGRS (Offender Group Reconviction Scale)
  • 2 to 3 alcohol-related violence incidents in their last 2 years in the community
Time to complete
Around 4 weeks

Healthy Identity Intervention

Extremism offence

Healthy Identity Intervention (HII) targets the social and psychological drivers of extremist offending. It helps participants disengage from extremist groups, causes, or ideologies and to reconnect with their own personal values and beliefs.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male or female
Risk criteria
22+ ERG (Extremism Risk Guidance) and an assessment
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes, but reviewed case by case
Time to complete
10 to 32 sessions (at least 3 months)

Identity Matters

Gang-related offence

Identity Matters (IM) is for men whose offending was motivated or enabled by their affiliation to a particular group. It aims to change the nature of their relationship to the group and helps them develop skills to resist peer influence.

Setting
Custody
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
  • Medium or high ROSH (Risk of Serious Harm)
  • 50+ OGRS (Offender Group Reconviction Scale)
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Assessed case by case
Time to complete
Flexible delivery with up to 23 sessions plus pre-programme session and programme review. Around 25 hours (at least 3 months).

Living as New Me

All offences

Living as New Me (LNM) is a booster or maintenance programme for men who have completed New Me Strengths or Becoming New Me Plus and may need extra support. In a custody setting, it is for men convicted of any offence. In the community, it is for sexual offences only.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
Medium or above risk
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Rolling programme with at least 5 sessions, often more

New Me MOT

All offences

New Me MOT (NMMOT) is not an Accredited Programme, but is for men who have already completed Becoming New Me Plus, Kaizen, Healthy Sex Programme, Horizon, iHorizon, New Me Strengths, the Cognitive Self-Change Programme or the Self-Change Programme. It offers support throughout a person's sentence, set out as a series of short exercises. These are completed with support from the perso's Prison Offender Manager in custody or their Probation Practitioner in the community. This gives them the chance to practise skills in different contexts, generalising and maintaining their learning from the Accredited Programme.

Setting
Custody or community
Gender
Male
Risk criteria
All risk scores
Suitable for people with learning disabilities or challenges (LDC)
Yes
Time to complete
Number of sessions is at the discretion of the practitioner and depends on the risk, need and responsivity profile of the individual. Has a 30-day Rehabilitation Activity Requirement (RAR) in the community.
